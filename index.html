RENEO VICENTE

Ecosystem Management Specialist

Bachelor of Science in Forestry
Nueva Viscaya State University

Major in Forest Resource Management

Job Responsibilities:

  1. Conduct assessments and studies on the implementation of Plans, Activities and Programs nationwide;
  2. Draft relevant policies and guidelines, and provide inputs to policies and guidelines prepared by other sectors of the DENR;
  3. Draft plans, activities, and programs for implementation in protected areas;
  4. Review Protected Area Management Plans and prepare draft affirmation memorandum to be signed by the DENR Secretary;
  5. Review applications for Special Use Agreement in Protected Areas, prepare final Terms and Condition of the agreement and prepare endorsement memorandum to the DENR Undersecretary for approval;
  6. Review reports of Protected Area Superintendents on biological, physical, social, cultural, and economic assessments, researches and studies;
  7. Conduct monitoring of the implementation of plans, activities and programs;
  8. Provide technical assistance to other government agencies and the private sector on the plans, activities and programs of the Bureau.

What I like Most of My Job:
I enjoy the perk of free travel enabling me to go to different places that I would not have been able to reach on my own. It is also exciting to work at the top level where I get to meet high officials in the DENR and some members of Congress. It is especially exciting when I get to appear at Senate consultations and when Senators call me by my name.

What I Like Least About My Job:
The mountain of papers that continue to accumulate in my desk, in every corner of my office cubicle. It is also does not help if you have a boss that adds to the stress.

Recommendations for those preparing to be Ecosystem Management Specialist:
Being an EMS is an exciting and rewarding job. Look at how the biodiversity around you is rapidly declining for you get challenged and take the call. If nobody will take ecosystem management and restoration, biodiversity conservation, and protection of natural heritage seriously, all will be lost in just a generation. The earth will get hotter, typhoons will get stronger and unpredictable, food will get the scarcer, and life will be miserable.
